Peregrine Falcon

Falco peregrinus

visibility Behavior

The Peregrine Falcon is renowned for its speed, reaching over 320 km/h (200 mph) during its characteristic hunting stoop (high-speed dive), making it the fastest bird in the world, as well as the fastest member of the animal kingdom. They are fierce defenders of their nest sites and have been known to attack much larger birds that wander too close.

restaurant Diet

Peregrine Falcons feed almost exclusively on medium-sized birds such as pigeons and doves, waterfowl, songbirds, and waders. In urban areas, the Rock Pigeon is the principal prey item. They catch their prey in mid-air with spectacular aerial maneuvers.

public Distribution

Cosmopolitan range (found everywhere except Antarctica)

The Peregrine Falcon has a breeding range that includes land regions from the Arctic tundra to the tropics. It can be found nearly everywhere on Earth, except extreme polar regions, very high mountains, and most tropical rainforests; the only major ice-free landmass from which it is entirely absent is New Zealand.
